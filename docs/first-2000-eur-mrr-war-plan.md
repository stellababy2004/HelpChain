# First EUR2,000 MRR War Plan

Internal founder execution plan for reaching the first EUR2,000 in monthly recurring revenue within 90 days.

This is not a brand plan. It is a sales operating plan. Revenue first, elegance second.

## 1. Define the Objective

Target: EUR2,000 MRR within 90 days.

### Practical Ways to Reach EUR2,000 MRR

| Mix | Revenue |
| --- | --- |
| 4 clients x EUR500/month | EUR2,000 MRR |
| 5 clients x EUR400/month | EUR2,000 MRR |
| 2 public clients x EUR750/month + 2 smaller orgs x EUR250/month | EUR2,000 MRR |
| 3 converted pilots x EUR500/month + 2 smaller pilots x EUR250/month | EUR2,000 MRR |

### Why First MRR Matters

- Proof: someone pays for the coordination problem HelpChain solves.
- Confidence: founder execution becomes grounded in buyer behavior, not assumptions.
- References: each paying client makes the next conversation easier.
- Compounding: outreach, demos, objections, pilots, and product priorities become sharper.

The first EUR2,000 MRR does not require a perfect product. It requires enough product credibility, a clear pain, a small buying step, and disciplined follow-up.

## 2. Best Fastest Customer Profile

Prioritize speed-to-close and pain clarity.

### Tier A: Fastest to Close

- Medium associations with coordination pain.
- Local centres sociaux.
- Agile directors who can decide without a long chain.
- Organizations where work is currently coordinated through email, Excel, phone, and personal memory.
- Teams that need visibility across requests, assignments, relances, and blocked cases.

Why Tier A first:

- Practical pain.
- Shorter decision path.
- Easier pilot scope.
- Better chance of paid pilot within 30 days.

### Tier B: Valuable but Slower

- CCAS with modern leadership.
- Municipal services with urgent operational need.
- Collectivites where one service can sponsor a narrow pilot.

Why Tier B matters:

- Higher credibility if converted.
- Strong reference value.
- Often more stakeholders and approval steps.

### Tier C: Avoid Early Unless Warm

- Large bureaucratic buyers.
- Public bodies with long procurement and unclear owner.
- Organizations demanding heavy custom work before a pilot.

Tier C can consume the whole quarter without producing MRR. Do not chase prestige before revenue.

## 3. 90-Day Breakdown

### Month 1: Build Pipeline, Demo Weekly, Secure First Pilot

Primary goal: create enough qualified conversations to find urgent pain.

Actions:

- Build a list of 100 Tier A and Tier B prospects.
- Send founder-led outreach every week.
- Book meetings with directors, coordinators, and operational owners.
- Run at least one live demo per week.
- Capture every objection.
- Offer the first paid pilot as soon as pain is clear.

Month 1 target:

- 60 outbound contacts.
- 30 follow-ups.
- 8 to 10 calls booked.
- 4 demos completed.
- 1 signed or verbally committed pilot.

### Month 2: Activate 2 to 3 Pilots and Tighten Messaging

Primary goal: turn interest into paid or conversion-ready usage.

Actions:

- Launch 2 to 3 pilots.
- Keep outreach running; do not pause sales because pilots exist.
- Tighten pitch around repeated objections.
- Show faster onboarding from access request to structure/admin setup.
- Start asking for referrals from every serious conversation.
- Convert first pilot to monthly recurring if proof appears before day 60.

Month 2 target:

- 2 to 3 active pilots.
- 1 to 2 paying clients or paid pilots.
- EUR500 to EUR1,000 MRR.
- Clear demo narrative and pilot offer.

### Month 3: Convert Pilots, Upsell Scope, Close Referrals

Primary goal: reach EUR2,000 MRR.

Actions:

- Hold pilot review meetings with decision-makers.
- Convert successful pilots to monthly subscriptions.
- Upsell seats, structures, or additional workflows only where usage proves value.
- Ask every active sponsor for one introduction.
- Use early proof to approach similar organizations.
- Push every warm account toward a decision date.

Month 3 target:

- 4 to 5 paying clients or paid pilots.
- EUR2,000 MRR.
- 2 usable references or private proof points.
- Clear next segment to scale.

## 7. Pilot Pricing Strategy

Charge enough to validate seriousness. Do not train the market to treat HelpChain as free consulting.

### Practical Early Offers

- 30-day pilot: EUR250 to EUR500.
- 60-day structured organization pilot: EUR500 to EUR1,000 total, or EUR300 to EUR600/month.
- Conversion after proof: monthly recurring subscription at EUR300 to EUR750/month depending on scope.

### Pricing Rules

- Monthly is fine early.
- Keep terms simple.
- Avoid complex annual contracts until proof exists.
- Discount only for fast decision, reference value, or narrow scope.
- Do not add custom features for free.
- Free pilot only if the reference value is explicit and strong.

### Conversion Trigger

At pilot review, ask:

"Did HelpChain make coordination clearer enough to continue next month?"

If yes: convert to monthly recurring.

If no: ask what blocked value and decide whether to fix, narrow, or close lost.

## 8. Existing HelpChain Tools to Use

### `/admin/organizations/requests`

Use as inbound institutional demand.

- Check daily.
- Prioritize requests from CCAS, centres sociaux, collectivites, and structured associations.
- Use request details and internal notes to prepare qualification.
- Move qualified requests quickly to demo or onboarding.

### `/admin/professional-leads`

Use as partner and referral pipeline.

- Filter by city, profession, status, and urgency.
- Identify people who can introduce institutions or validate workflows.
- Treat professional leads as both prospects and channels.

### `/admin/audience-map`

Use as market signal.

- Watch top pages and high-intent paths.
- Look for territorial concentration.
- Use page interest to shape outreach angle.
- If `/deploiement` or `/demander-acces` is active, push pilot-focused outreach.

### Revenue Radar

Use as prioritization, not magic identification.

- Review hot sessions daily.
- Prioritize repeated high-intent sessions.
- Look for source patterns like Google, LinkedIn, or direct repeat visits.
- Increase outreach in segments matching the hottest behavior.

### Prospect Auto Capture

Use as conversion context.

- On access request or lead details, read "Audience avant conversion".
- Use viewed pages to personalize the first reply.
- If a prospect viewed `/offre` and `/deploiement`, speak directly about pilot scope.
- If a prospect viewed `/professionnels`, consider partner/referral angle.

## 10. What to Stop Doing

Stop immediately:

- Endless feature building without a named buyer.
- Logo polishing.
- Passive waiting for inbound.
- Chasing impossible large buyers early.
- Over-customizing for free.
- Writing strategy instead of sending outreach.

- Treating traffic as progress without conversion action.

## 11. If Behind Target at Day 30 / 60

### If Behind at Day 30

Problem signs:

- Fewer than 4 demos completed.
- No serious pilot opportunity.
- Low reply volume.
- Product work has displaced outreach.

Corrective actions:

- Double outreach volume for two weeks.
- Contact 30 prospects per week instead of 15.
- Call after email for all Tier A prospects.
- Ask every warm contact for one introduction.
- Narrow the pitch to one pain: requests, assignments, relances, visibility.
- Stop non-critical product work until demos are booked.

### If Behind at Day 60

Problem signs:

- Fewer than 2 pilots active.
- No paid pilot.
- Demos are friendly but not converting.
- Buyers say "interesting" but no decision date exists.

Corrective actions:

- Raise urgency in the close: propose a 30-day pilot with a specific review date.
- Ask directly: "What would need to be true for you to test this next month?"
- Use any positive pilot feedback as private proof in outreach.
- Offer narrow paid pilots at EUR250 to EUR500.
- Push referrals from every warm conversation.
- Remove prospects with no decision path from the active pipeline.
