# First Scenario — Coordinating a Social Request

## Context

A local structure receives a request concerning an elderly person living alone. The person has difficulty leaving home, has missed several essential appointments, and now needs rapid social support coordination. The request must be reviewed, assigned, followed, and closed without losing visibility on who is doing what.

## Step 1 — Request Intake

An authorized user creates the request in HelpChain. The request includes the essential operational information: location, type of need, urgency level, and a short description of the situation. Once recorded, the request becomes visible in the system for the teams allowed to handle it.

## Step 2 — Qualification and Assignment

An operator or administrator reviews the request, checks whether the information is sufficient, and confirms the appropriate level of urgency. The request is then assigned to the responsible actor, for example a social worker, a partner association, or the relevant local structure. These actions are controlled: only authorized users can qualify, assign, or change the request.

## Step 3 — Operational Follow-up

The request moves through a clear lifecycle, for example from open to in progress and then to done. During follow-up, the responsible team records notes, updates, and key actions taken. This creates a continuous operational record rather than a scattered exchange of emails or phone calls.

## Step 4 — Coordination Between Actors

Several actors may contribute to the same request. One structure may qualify the situation, another may provide field support, and a professional may confirm the outcome. HelpChain keeps the request in one controlled workflow, which helps avoid duplicate handling, conflicting instructions, or loss of information. Visibility remains controlled according to role and scope.

## Step 5 — Resolution and Closure

Once the required support has been delivered and the situation has been resolved, the request is marked as done. Operationally, this means the responsible team has completed the necessary follow-up and the case no longer requires active handling. The system keeps a record of the full lifecycle, including assignment, updates, and closure.

## Step 6 — Reporting and Oversight

The structure can then review its operational activity through the system. It can see how many requests were handled, how requests are distributed across statuses, and how activity is evolving over time. This supports day-to-day supervision as well as broader operational decision-making.

## What This Scenario Demonstrates

This scenario shows how HelpChain supports coordinated handling of a social request from intake to closure. It demonstrates controlled assignment, structured follow-up, traceable actions, role-based visibility, and a shared operational view of the work in progress.

## Key Principles

- One request follows one controlled lifecycle.
- Actions are recorded and remain traceable.
- Access is role-based and scoped to the relevant actors.
- Coordination replaces informal and fragmented communication.
- Operational follow-up is structured from intake to closure.
- Activity data supports oversight and decision-making.
